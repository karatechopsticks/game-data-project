Hotline Miami 2: Wrong Number is an upcoming 2D top-down action video game, developed by Dennaton Games and published by Devolver Digital. A sequel to Hotline Miami, Wrong Number takes place before and after the events of Hotline Miami, and focuses on the aftermath of the protagonist Jacket after killing off the entire Russian mafia on the behalf of mysterious messages on his phone.[1]

Hotline Miami 2 will be the final game of the series.[2]

Gameplay[edit]

Gameplay will function mainly like in the previous game, but Hotline Miami 2 will feature a new "hard mode", which can only be unlocked when the player finishes a level with a ranking of C+ or higher. In "hard mode", enemies will be more difficult to take down and some abilities may be taken away from the player, such as "enemy-locking".[3]

Each character features their own abilities; Corey can do a roll move, Mark has two weapons and can spread his arms to shoot left and right simultaneously, Alex and Ash have a chainsaw and a gun respectively.[4]

A level editor option has also been announced.[5]

Story[edit]

Hotline Miami 2 will take place before and after the events of the original, focusing more on the latter. The game suggests that Jacket's interpretation ending is the canonical end of the first game. As the main protagonist, Jacket is killing off the Russian mob for the Janitors, a movement of masked vigilantes is started and scours across Miami, Florida. The story is told through different factions of characters.[2]

The game features different stories, with multiple interpretations:

  • The Pig Butcher: Following the events of Jacket's massacre, he is now depicted as a cold-blooded serial killer called the "Pig Butcher" in a fictional slasher film based on the events of the first game known as "Midnight Animal".[6]
  • The Fans: Though most people view Jacket as a murderer, the "Fans" believe he is a hero, and want to follow in his steps and receive the same phone calls. However, Jacket killed off the entire Russian mob, meaning the Fans can no longer directly follow in his steps because the Janitors no longer have a reason to call. The objective for the Fans is to kill off random thugs they find, in order to get media attention, hoping that the mysterious calls will reappear.[6] The Fans symbolize those who wish for Hotline Miami 2 to play like the original game. Like the original, players will receive phone calls, kill thugs, and collect masks.[7]
  • The Detective: A playable character without any unique attributes, the "Detective" can start a level with a shotgun found inside his trunk. First seen in a diner talking to a waitress, whenever he finishes a level, he meets with police officers. He plays much like Jacket from the first Hotline Miami.[8]
  • The Redneck: A large man with a choice of snake masks. Not much has been revealed yet apart from his membership in 50 Blessings and a desire to know where the phone calls are coming from. Also appears to have a hatred of Russians as revealed in his visit to a Russian tattoo parlor.[9]
  • The Mobster: A Russian mobster with no masks, similar to The Detective. His segments start in the past, presumably during the events of the first game. Seems to be held in high regards by the Russian Mob Boss, but wants to get out of the game. He doesn't appear to use masks, but has a wide arsenal available to him.
  • The Writer: A crime writer investigating the story behind the mask killings with connections to The Detective. Contrary to the other characters, The Writer doesn't seem to outright kill people; many of his attacks are nonlethal unless the player performs a 'finisher' on a downed enemy. Repeated violence causes his vision to turn red and his attacks to become deadly. Normally, however, The Writer will unload any firearm he picks up and will aim to disable his opponents, usually by beating them with blunt objects.

Hotline Miami 2 Demo Reaction[edit]

The demo shown at Rezzed and the 2013 Penny Arcade Expo featured gameplay in the tutorial that had players appear to attempt to sexually assault a woman as the Pig Butcher. The player character lowered his pants and straddled the woman before the scene is interrupted by the director of Midnight Animal, revealing the whole sequence to be fake. Video game journalists, including Cara Ellison of PC Gamer, spoke out against the usage of sexual assault imagery.[10] In response, Dennis Wedin stated that Dennaton cut the scene from the demo, and that they were reconsidering putting the scene into the final game. Wedin also stated that they cut the scene short to show that that type of violence is not what the Hotline Miami series is about.[11]
