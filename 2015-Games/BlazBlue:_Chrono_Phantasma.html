BlazBlue: Chrono Phantasma, released in Japan as BlazBlue: Chronophantasma (ブレイブルー クロノファンタズマ, BureiBurū Kuronofantazuma?), is a 2-D fighting game developed by Arc System Works. It is the third game of the Blazblue series, set after the events of BlazBlue: Continuum Shift. The game was originally to be released first as an arcade game in the fourth quarter of 2012,[3] which was later pushed forward to November 2012.[4] A PlayStation 3 version of the game was released in Japan on October 24, 2013,[5] while it was released in the United States on March 25, 2014. Due to limited hardware and disc space the game was not released on the Xbox 360.[6] An updated version of the game titled BlazBlue: Chrono Phantasma Extend was released on the PlayStation 3, PlayStation Vita, PlayStation 4 and Xbox One in April 2015.[7] It is currently scheduled for release in Summer 2015 in North America.[8]

Changes from Continuum Shift[edit]

Besides normal rebalancing and move updates, Chrono Phantasma features several revamped gameplay mechanics and features five new characters: Bullet, Azrael, Amane Nishiki, Izayoi, and Kagura Mutsuki. In addition to these five are two new downloadable characters, Yuuki Terumi and Kokonoe. Two additional characters, Celica A. Mercury and Lambda-11, will be added to the arcade version of Chrono Phantasma in a future update. The character sprites have also been redrawn and refined and several new songs were composed for the game. All previous musical themes have been re-arranged, and new songs have been added. The storyline will also pick up after the events of Continuum Shift.[9]

A new mechanic called "Overdrive" has been added to the game, replacing the Gold Bursts from Continuum Shift. Green Bursts are still in but are the only form of Burst available. Distortion Drives such as Ragna's "Blood Kain", Taokaka's "Almost Becoming Two!", Litchi's "Great Wheel" and Bang's "Fu-Rin-Ka-Zan" have been retooled to be their respective characters' Overdrives. The length of an Overdrive increases the lower a character's health is, and performing an Overdrive stops the round timer while it is active.

Guard Primers have been removed entirely and the Guard Crush has been moved to a new mechanic called "Crush Trigger" where, at the cost of 25 Heat, a player may hit the A and B attack buttons simultaneously to cause a Guard Crush against blocking opponents, provided the opponent is not using Barrier Block.

Characters[edit]

Main article: List of BlazBlue characters

All 19 characters from BlazBlue: Continuum Shift Extend return in Chrono Phantasma, with the exception of Lambda-11 (until Extend), who is replaced by Nu-13. This brings the base roster to 24 playable characters with two extra characters that can be purchased off the PlayStation Store.

New characters include Amane Nishiki, an effeminate young man clad in a kimono; Bullet, a mercenary who seeks revenge on Sector Seven for the death of her squad; Azrael, a fist-fighting soldier of Sector Seven; Izayoi, the true form of Tsubaki Yayoi after the full release of the Sealed Weapon Izayoi's powers; and Kagura Mutsuki, the head of one of the most powerful Duodecim families and one of the NOL's greatest generals. The DLC characters consist of Yuuki Terumi, one of the Six Heroes and the true form of Hazama, and Kokonoe, a genius scientist of Sector Seven and the daughter of two of the Six Heroes, Jubei and Nine.[5][10][11]

Chrono Phantasma Extend adds two additional characters: Lambda-11 and Celica A. Mercury.[citation needed]

Sales[edit]

BlazBlue: Chrono Phantasma sold over 72,000 copies in its first week on sale, making it the best debut in the BlazBlue series so far.[12]

Chrono Phantasma has received positive reviews upon release with critics citing the overhauled fighting mechanics as an improvement over Calamity Trigger and Continuum Shift, seven new unique fighters, smooth online play, in-depth training and tutorial modes, and an engaging story. Criticism is still directed toward the character designs and confusion of playing story mode without past knowledge of previous games.
