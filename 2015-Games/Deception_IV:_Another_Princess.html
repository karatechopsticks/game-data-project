Deception IV: Another Princess, known in Japan as Kagero: Another Princess (影牢 ～もう1人のプリンセス～) is a strategy game developed by Tecmo Koei. Deception IV: Another Princess is an updated version of Deception IV: Blood Ties, which was originally released on the 27 February 2014 in Japan.[1]

Deception IV: Another Princess is the first Deception game to be released on the PlayStation 4; it was also available on the PlayStation 3 and PlayStation Vita on the 26 March 2015.[2]

Gameplay[edit]

Another Princess introduces the new character Valgyrie, who is able to set traps as well as directly attack enemies by kicking.[3] The player can deal damage to an enemy by stomping on them after they have fallen to the ground, or kick them into traps.[4]

Modes[edit]

The main feature of the game is called the Quest Mode, which features 100 new quests to complete.[1][4] In it, the player plays as Valgyrie, and is able to obtain new traps and abilities.[5]

The game will also include an option titled Deception Studio, which allows players to create their own characters, enemies, and missions.[4] Parts for Deception Studio can be acquired by playing Quest Mode.[4]

Characters[edit]

Another Princess will include multiple characters. Some appear as enemies at first in the quest mode, but are playable after the player completes certain quests.[6]

  • Valgyrie (ヴァルギリエ, Varugirye?) is the second daughter of the devil and the protagonist of Deception IV: Another Princess. She is described as the "nightmare princess" that humans will see in their dreams. She has lost her original powers due to a long sleep.
  • Laegrinna (レグリナ, Regurina?) is the first daughter of the devil and first appeared in Deception IV: Blood Ties. She has very light skin and snow white hair. In comparison to her sister Valgyrie, Laegrinna does not physically hit her victims, but disposes of them with traps.
  • Allura (アリシア, Arishia?)[6] is the princess of Fronenberg and first appeared in Trapt. She was accused of murdering the king by her stepmother Catalina.
  • Reina (レイナ?)[6] is a girl from Burganfada and first appeared in Deception III: Dark Delusion. She was kidnapped by an assassin named Miguel and was sent to Alendar. The king of Alendar, king Federick, sent Reina to Hades Jail, where a boy named Marco gave her the power to use traps so that she would not be harmed.
  • Millennia (ミレニア, Mirenia?)[6] first appeared in Kagero: Deception II. As a child she wandered into a mansion, chasing an orb of light that made her feel happy. Over the years of being in the mansion she was trained in the ways of using traps by a Timenoid called Yocal.

Development[edit]

This section requires expansion. (February 2015)

Tecmo Koei announced Deception IV: Another Princess for Playstation 4, Playstation 3, and Playstation Vita in an issue of the Famitsu magazine on the 12 December 2014.[1] At the time of the announcement, development was reported to be 50% complete.[1]

Promotion[edit]

Players that pre-order the Japanese version of Deception IV: Another Princess will receive the 'Golden Toilet' trap, a golden version of one of the new traps that are in Deception IV: Another Princess.[7]

Players that buy the treasure box edition of Deception IV: Another Princess will receive three exclusive traps.[7]
