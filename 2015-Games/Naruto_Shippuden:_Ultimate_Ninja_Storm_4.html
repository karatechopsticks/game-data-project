Naruto Shippuden: Ultimate Ninja Storm 4, known in Japan as Naruto Shippuden: Narutimate Storm 4 (ナルト- 疾風伝ナルティメットストーム 4, Naruto Shippūden: Narutimetto Sutōmu 4?)[1] the sixth installment of Ultimate Ninja Storm is an upcoming fighting game developed by CyberConnect2 and published by Bandai Namco Entertainment for Microsoft Windows, PlayStation 4 and Xbox One as part of the Naruto: Ultimate Ninja series, based on the manga Naruto by Masashi Kishimoto. It is the sequel to Naruto Shippuden: Ultimate Ninja Storm 3, and will be the first Naruto: Ultimate Ninja Storm installment for PlayStation 4 and Xbox One. It will cover the finale of the series therefore making it the last installment in the series.[2]

Gameplay[edit]

Returning to this game from previous installments is the "Wall Running" feature where two shinobi are able to fight while climbing the walls of the selected stage. Unlike previous installments however, where if one player ran up the wall the other player was forced to fight on the wall as well, in this game it is implied that if one character runs up the wall the other character can still fight on the ground. The option to select one of the three fighting types from Storm Revolution is removed. Just like its predecessors, the player can use all three types at once during a single and/or team battle. A new feature will be introduced in the game called "Leader Swap", where you can switch out the main character you're fighting as to fight against your opponent with your support characters. It is unknown as of this time if the character switched in will share the same life bar as the main character or not. Also, in boss battles, you can damage the opponent high enough to break their armor or weapon. Also the different from the Clash of Ninja series.

Development[edit]

Despite its title, the "Ultimate Ninja Storm" series is not a continuation of the Naruto: Clash of Ninja series. Development for this game, Hiroshi Matsuyama stated that he wanted the game's life to be as long as possible. In order to achieve this, he and his team made significant changes to the game's combat mechanics, which will include a new system of strategy developed exclusively for the game and changes to the game's internal combat and damage system. The team has also taken on board the feedback given from the Naruto gaming community and has focused on the addition of more story based elements, rather than the traditional jump from fight to fight. The game is set to feature the largest number of fighters in the series history. That roster includes characters from the fight between Hashirama and Madara, as well as characters from The Last: Naruto the Movie. The game will also recreate the Shinobi World War.[3]
