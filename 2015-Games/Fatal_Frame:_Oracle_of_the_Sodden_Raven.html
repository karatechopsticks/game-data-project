Fatal Frame: Oracle of the Sodden Raven (零 〜濡鴉ノ巫女〜?, lit. "Zero: The Black Haired Shrine Maiden"),[4][5] informally known as Fatal Frame V in North America and Project Zero 5 in Europe, is a video game for the Wii U developed by Koei Tecmo released on September 27, 2014 in Japan. It is the fifth Fatal Frame game in the series, the prior being Zero: Tsukihami no Kamen.[6][7]

During a live presentation via Nico Nico on July 17, 2014, both Koei Tecmo and Nintendo revealed the initial details of the game along with the first trailer and screenshots.[8] In a Nintendo Direct presentation on April 1, 2015, a Western release for the game was confirmed in both Europe and North America, though its localised titles were not confirmed.[3][2]

Gameplay[edit]

Further information: Fatal Frame § Series gameplay

Like in previous games in the series, the player must defeat hostile ghosts by taking photographs of them. The Wii U GamePad is used like a real camera, and extra damage can be dealt to enemies by rotating the controller before taking a shot.[9]

Setting[edit]

The game takes place in a large open environment with mountains, lakes, and graveyards.[1] The mountain setting is constantly rainy and has many flowing water features, and sopping-wet ghosts haunt the area.[10][1]

Development[edit]

In-game visuals showing Yuuri.

The game details were first announced on April 24, 2014 by Koei Tecmo exclusively for the Wii U.[11] The game didn't make an appearance at E3 2014. However, later Nintendo announced on Twitter a special live broadcast for the upcoming game.[12]

During the live broadcast via Nico Nico, the game's producer, Keisuke Kikuchi, made an appearance along with Shigeru Miyamoto to talk about the game, which was revealed as Zero: Nuregarasu no Miko.[13][1]

The game utilises engine technology from Dead or Alive 5.[14]

Reception[edit]

Reception
Review scores
Publication Score
Famitsu 33/40[15]

Famitsu magazine awarded Fatal Frame: Oracle of the Sodden Raven a score of 33/40 based on four reviews (7/8/9/9). Each reviewer agreed that using the GamePad like a real camera enhanced the game's atmosphere and that it was quite scary, but one reviewer felt the controls were sluggish.[15]
