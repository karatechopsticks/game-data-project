Age of Empires: Castle Siege is a free-to-play medieval MMO tower defense game in the form of a Windows app, designed for Windows 8.1 and Windows Phone. It was developed by Smoking Gun Interactive and Microsoft Studios, and was formally announced on August 26, 2014. Unlike any preceding Age of Empires game, Castle Siege takes the form of a tower defense game.[1]

Gameplay[edit]

In Age of Empires: Castle Siege the player builds a castle for themself to produce resources and to raise an army. The army is used to attack other players' castles in order to loot their resources an to earn crowns, the game's scorekeeping mechanism. Players can also form alliances with each other for various social functions.

Buildings[edit]

The castle is built around a keep, surrounded by buildings of various types. Economy buildings produce and store resources, while military buildings are used to train armies. The castle is protected from enemy attacks by fortifications, including walls, towers and various traps. Players can also build decorative constructions that serve no practical purposes. All buildings can be updated to increase their production and to gain additional perks. Updating the keep increases the amount of buildings allowed and makes additional building types available.

Resources[edit]

There are three types of resources for construction: food, wood and stone. Gold is an in-game currency that can be used to speed up building processes or to buy other resources. Gold can be purchased with real-life currency. The player's base also produces pennants, used to recruit hero units and special troops.

Troops[edit]

The armies consist of three unit types: infantry, cavalry and siege engines. Each civilization also has their own special unit; these are more powerful than the basic units, but also require more resources to recruit. The player can also include up to two hero units in each assault. The heroes are the most powerful units in the game and they all have some special ability. All units can be upgrded to make them more powerful.

Battle[edit]

Players use their armies to attack each other's castles. The player has five minutes to break down the defenses and destroy as many buildings as they can. A victory is achieved if the attacker is able to destroy the keep or at least half of the buildings. Resources can be looted by attacking storage buildings.
