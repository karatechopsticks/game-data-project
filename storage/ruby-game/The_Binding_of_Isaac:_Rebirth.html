The Binding of Isaac: Rebirth is an upcoming remake of The Binding of Isaac developed by Nicalis and designed by Edmund McMillen.

Development[edit]

The Binding of Isaac: Rebirth is a remake of the original The Binding of Isaac. It is produced and developed by Nicalis, and designed by Edmund McMillen.[1] Nicalis is developing it for the PlayStation 4, PlayStation Vita, and Steam.[1] Nicalis was in talks with Microsoft and Nintendo relating to the possibility of a release for their consoles.[1] An iOS version of the game was a possibility "if it's not garbage."[1] McMillen expressed an interest in seeing it released for the Nintendo 3DS.[2] The game will feature local two-player co-op but not online co-op to avoid delays.[1] The soundtrack will be composed by Matthias Bossi and Jon Evans, and features remixes of music from the original game.[1] The graphics will be changed to a 16-bit style due to McMillen's distaste for the original's art style.[1] A poll was conducted by McMillen to gauge interest by fans of which art style they preferred.[3] It will include all of the content from The Binding of Isaac and its Wrath of the Lamb expansion, as well as new content that is the same size as the content added with Wrath of the Lamb. It includes a new final chapter and ending, at least three new playable characters, and "tons more items, rooms, enemies, bosses and the like."[1][4] Edmund intends to make the game something that people who played the original would want to play.[1] Some of the new content would be content cut from the original version due to the limitations of the Flash engine.[1]

In January 2014, Edmund announced on his blog that the development of preexisting content had finished and that they were now moving onto the development of new content. At this time, he also said that development was "beyond the half point."[4][5]

Edmund McMillen stated that the primary purpose of the remake will be to take it out of Flash and to use a new engine to help limit the game's bugs. It will feature re-balanced items and enemies, 16-bit graphics, 2 player local co-op support, new items, new enemies, console support[6] and Linux support.[7] The game was originally supposed to be on the PlayStation 3.[8]

During Sony's 2013 Gamescom press conference in August 2013, it was announced that the game will be coming to the PlayStation 4 and PlayStation Vita; the title will be available to PlayStation Plus customers at no additional cost. The game will also be available for the PC via Steam. It is expected for release in 2014.[9] On the same day, Nicalis tweeted that there was still a chance that a Nintendo 3DS version will happen.[10][11] When Edmund was asked if there was a good chance for a 3DS port, he replied with "nope".[12]

On the 5th of September 2014, Valve announced that The Binding of Isaac: Rebirth was available for pre-order on the PC platform via Steam.[13] A 33% discount on "Rebirth" applies to owners of the original "Binding of Isaac" on Steam, reordering this will give you a promo item for team fortress two. This offer expires at the game's release (10AM Pacific Time on November the 4th, 2014.)

Reception[edit]

The poll conducted by McMillen about the 16-bit style found that 37.5% of voters liked it, 53.1% were either fine with it or did not care either way, and 9.5% hated it.[2]
