Gunman Clive is a indie side-scrolling Western action video game developed and published by Sweden independent video game developer Hörberg Productions. It was released for the Nintendo 3DS (via the 3DS eShop), Microsoft Windows (via Steam), and iPhone and Android mobile platforms (via the App Store and Google Play respectively) on January 3, 2013. The game follows a lone gunslinger in a futuristic Wild West as he tries to save the mayor's daughter from a group of technologically well-armed bandits.

Gameplay[edit]

The player controls the titular Gunman Clive as he fights different sets of enemies across 16 levels which progress and add difficulty by each level. The player has to traverse along a 2D platformer filled with moving platforms, traps and enemies.[1] Shooting is a huge emphasis in the game, and the player can pick up specialized weapon such as a spread shot, explosive bullets, and a laser gun. Enemies vary in each level, from bandits who carry rifles and dynamites to animals such as pelicans and wolves. Aside from the occasional grunts, bosses can also be found in the game. A life meter runs out every time the player takes damage.[2]

The 2D graphics has the animation style similar of a sketch, while the backgrounds are of the same color of brown typical of the Wild West. Gunman Clive's soundtrack is described as "old west authenticity",[2] paying tribute to the Western genre. The game offers two playable characters; Gunman Clive and the Ms. Johnson. The only difference between the two is that the latter is slower and has a skirt that can slow down decent.[1] A third playable character is unlocked after completion of the game. While Gunman Clive has three difficulty settings, the difference between each is how much damage you take and where you respawn after every death.

Plot[edit]

In the year of 18XX, the west is overrun by thugs and outlaws. A group of bandits have kidnapped Mayor Johnson's daughter and are spreading havok across the land, and it's up to Gunman Clive to bring order to the west and save the girl.

Gunman Clive received critical acclaim after its release from both mainstream and video game journalists. The gameplay, art and style has been critically praised, although many noted that its major flaw is its short length.[2] Many critics also noted that the game pays homage to classic platformers such as Mega Man, Meat Boy and Super Mario.[2] Destructoid described it as "a steal at $2" and that "fans of 2D action platformers need to check it out. If Hörberg Productions is ever graced with the opportunity to develop a mainline Mega Man game, I'm confident that fans of the series would be happy with the results."[2]

IGN described it as "a brief experience, but still one well worth experiencing if you’re looking for a fine $2 platformer on your 3DS. The stellar game design is emboldened by the three different characters you can play as. From start to finish, Gunman Clive is a fantastic platformer, good enough that it is worth playing multiple times to play as every character."[1]

By January 2015, more than 400,000 copies of the game had been sold, with over eighty percent of sales on the 3DS.[7]

Sequel[edit]

A sequel has been announced to be in development. The story now takes Clive around the world to chase after the leader of the bandits from the first game. A trailer was also released by Nintendo.[8] It was released on January 29, 2015.[9]
